Our Founder

Foyle’s commitment to Democracy Matters has been widely hailed in the press, including in The New York Times, Sports Illusterated, USA Today and more. Foyle is passionate about activating young people to be civically and politically engaged. In an op-ed piece entitled “My Generation Is Far From Apathetic,” Foyle recounted the tremendous energy and creativity Democracy Matters students have brought to the work of deepening democracy.

I started DM campus chapters to inspire students to stand up to the big money interests corrupting our democracy and to fight against voter suppression. I believe students can be a powerful voice in the pro-democracy movement sweeping the country.

The NBA has recognized Foyle three times for his commitment to democracy with nationally televised presentations of their “Community Assist Award.” The Greenlining Institute presented him with their prestigious Change Agent Award, and he received the City of San Francisco’s Sports Hero Award. Most recently, he was inducted into the Sports Humanitarian Hall of Fame.

Foyle has spoken about Democracy Matters at hundreds of high schools, universities, and civic associations including The American Bar Association, the Law School of the University of California, and the New York Conference on Campaign Finance Reform and Communities of Color. He frequently speaks to classes on college campuses about his passion for getting big money out of politics and people back in with voting reform..

Staff

Executive Director Joan Mandle, Ph.D.
joanm@democracymatters.org

Joan Mandle has been the Executive Director of Democracy Matters since 2001. She is also Associate Professor of Sociology and Anthropology, Emerita, at Colgate University, where she directed the college’s successful Women’s Studies Program and founded and supervised Colgate’s Center for Women’s Studies. Her teaching and her extensive published work explore social change and social movements. She has published books on the women’s movement, women’s studies programs, and with Jay R. Mandle, Change Elections to Change America: Democracy Matters Students in Action.

From her years as a student political activist, Mandle has been actively involved in progressive social movements and efforts to reform the political system. She spearheaded grassroots organizing drives in Oakland, California and San Francisco that resulted in passage of landmark campaign finance legislation. Mandle also served as Campaign Manager for incumbent Congressman Robert W. Edgar of Pennsylvania for two election cycles.

Professor Mandle has received widespread recognition for her political work, including the League of Women Voters of Oakland’s “Civic Contribution Award,” the Sociologists for Women in Society’s “Feminist Activism Award” for a lifetime of service and activism, and the Progressive Leadership Award from Citizen Action of New York.

National Field Director Anita Moore
anitam@democracymatters.org

Anita is driven by a sense of urgency in our current political system to work for the rights of citizens through social justice and activism. She has worked with migrant, Native, and underrepresented communities in North America, advocating for equal access and a political voice in these times of hardship. She received her degree in Masters of Social Justice in Intercultural Relations from SIT Graduate Institute in Brattleboro, Vermont, and presently lives in the heart of North Carolina, her home state. Anita is a musician and artist, integrating these tools in her activism work. She also teaches critical thinking at Alamance Community College.
